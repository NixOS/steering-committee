## Old topics (20 min)

- AI policy (@roberth)
  - No textual update from @roberth; previous feedback was to make it more outcome-oriented.
  - @cafkafk: proposes reframing from an "AI policy" to a "quality policy". For her, LLM usage is primarily a provenance / license-compliance concern. A quality bar applies equally to humans and to AI output and avoids an ideological debate about "how much AI".
  - @tomberek: current draft does not contradict this; suggests expanding the preamble to make clear the principles apply to any source, not just AI. Raised two failure modes: (1) discussions degenerating into "this is slop" / "no it isn't", (2) the unaddressed case of LLM-to-LLM interaction ("dreams talking to dreams").
  - @roberth: current draft already proposes forbidding direct contributor communication through LLMs, while leaving room for maintainer teams to opt into AI review tooling. On disclosure: personally indifferent, finds the disclosure framing not very outcome-oriented.
  - @tomberek: disclosure is still a useful social convention (signals "take with a grain of salt"); keep it until better norms emerge.
  - @Ericson2314: not putting "AI" in the title could be savvy; @cafkafk: but some people will read that as the SC dodging the topic — naming it "AI policy" while scoping it around quality may be the right balance.
  - @JulienMalka: would like to collaborate with the new community bootstrap team so ownership is shared rather than unilaterally decided by the SC.
  - @Ericson2314: worries about punting indefinitely and about using the moderation team as an opening "bargaining position" that pushes the policy more anti-LLM than Robert's draft. Wants the SC to actually decide, not just delegate.
  - @cafkafk: concerned about friction between the incoming moderation team (leaning more anti-LLM) and the SC (generally the other way); wants to de-risk that conflict while still engaging the right people.
  - Edge case discussed: an LLM-driven automation (e.g. an ofBorg/Ryantm-style version bumper) contributed by a maintainer like @Mic92. Consensus: boilerplate / mechanical changes are fine; unsupervised generation of *new logic* is not — this is exactly the kind of case a quality policy covers naturally.
  - Action: @roberth to open a PR for the draft so edits can be suggested inline; members to propose concrete edits there.
